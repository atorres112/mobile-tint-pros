This guide explains Illinois window tint laws in simple terms based on 625 ILCS 5/12-503. It is meant to help vehicle owners understand what is legal and illegal regarding window tinting in Illinois.

## Front Windshield
- Tint is only allowed on the top 6 inches of the windshield.
- Tint must be non-reflective.
- Anything else on the windshield is illegal.

## Front Side Windows (Driver and Passenger)
- If rear windows are darker than 30%, front windows must allow at least 50% light.
- If rear windows are darker than 35%, front windows must allow at least 35% light.
- Factory tinted rear windows allow 50% tint on front windows.

## Rear Windows
- Any darkness is allowed.
- Decorative or perforated films are allowed if side mirrors are installed.

## Medical Exemption
- Allowed with doctor certification.
- Must carry paperwork in the vehicle.
- Must renew every 4 years.
- Installer must keep records.

## What Is Not Allowed
- Reflective tint on the windshield or front windows.
- Stickers or objects blocking view.
- Tint darker than legal limits.
- Tint without medical documentation.

## Penalties
- First offense: $50 to $500 fine.
- Repeat offense: Class C misdemeanor.
- Vehicle must be brought into compliance.

## Dealer Rules
- Dealers cannot allow test drives with illegal tint.
- Vehicles must be compliant before driving.
